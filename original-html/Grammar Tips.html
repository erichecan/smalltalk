Grammar Tips

Verb Tenses

Present Perfect

Use the present perfect to talk about actions that started in the past and continue to the present.

Example:

'I have lived in London for five years.'

Past Simple

Use the past simple to talk about completed actions in the past.

Example:

'I visited Paris last summer.'

Sentence Structure

SVO Order

A basic English sentence follows the Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) order.

Example:

'She (Subject) reads (Verb) books (Object).'

Conjunctions

Use conjunctions like 'and,' 'but,' 'or,' 'so,' 'because' to connect clauses.

Example:

'I like tea, but I prefer coffee.'

Prepositions

In, On, At

Use 'in' for enclosed spaces, 'on' for surfaces, and 'at' for specific points.

Example:

'The book is on the table. I am in the room. Meet me at the corner.'

To

Use 'to' to indicate direction or destination.

Example:

'I am going to the store.'
